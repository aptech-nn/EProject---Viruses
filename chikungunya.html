Chikungunya

Chikungunya is a viral disease transmitted to humans by infected mosquitoes. It was first introduced during an outbreak in southern Tanzania in 1952. The symptoms include abrupt onset of fever accompanied by muscle pain, headache, nausea, fatigue, rash, and severe joint pain. Sometimes the symptoms are mild, and the infection go unrecognized or misdiagnosed as dengue fever.
There is currently no specific antiviral drug treatment regime and no commercial chikungunya vaccine. Treatment is directed primarily at relieving the symptoms, using antipyretics, analgesics and fluids. Most cases recover completely within weeks.
Prevention and control are usually done by destroying or reducing natural and artificial mosquito breeding habitats, and by using insecticides to control mosquitoes. Using insecticide-treated mosquito nets and wearing clothing that minimizes exposure to mosquito bites can be helpful.

Aspect Information
What is Chikungunya? A viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes, primarily Aedes aegypti.
First Outbreak Occurred in southern Tanzania in 1952.
Symptoms Fever, muscle pain, joint pain, headache, rash, and nausea.
Diagnosis Often confused with dengue fever. Diagnosis is confirmed through laboratory tests.
Treatment No specific antiviral treatment. Symptom management with pain relievers, fluids, and rest.
Prevention Use insecticides, wear protective clothing, and eliminate mosquito breeding sites.
Vaccine No commercial vaccine available as of now.
How is chikungunya spread?
Chikungunya is a viral illness spread by the bite of infected mosquitoes.
What are the symptoms?
Chikungunya causes severe joint pain that can last for a long time.
How is chikungunya treated?
Chikungunya causes severe joint pain that can last for a long time.
How can chikungunya be prevented?
Chikungunya can be prevented by avoiding mosquito bites and eliminating mosquito breeding sites.
How can I protect myself from chikungunya?
Wear long sleeves and pants, use mosquito repellent, and use mosquito nets.
Can chikungunya be passed to a baby?
Yes, a pregnant woman with chikungunya can pass it to her baby during the second trimester or during birth.
