1976 - First Ebola Outbreak

The Ebola virus was first identified in 1976 during outbreaks in Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of Congo) and Sudan.

  • First recorded cases of Ebola hemorrhagic fever.
  • High mortality rate with over 280 deaths.

1995 - Kikwit Outbreak

A significant outbreak occurred in Kikwit, Democratic Republic of Congo, with a mortality rate of 81%.

  • Over 250 reported deaths.
  • Improved containment measures introduced.

2014 - West Africa Epidemic

The deadliest Ebola outbreak occurred in West Africa, affecting Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone.

  • More than 11,000 deaths recorded.
  • WHO declared Ebola a global health emergency.

2018 - DRC Outbreak

An Ebola outbreak occurred in the Democratic Republic of Congo, leading to a significant response effort.

  • Experimental vaccines were used for the first time.
  • WHO and health agencies worked on rapid containment.

2024 - Ongoing Efforts

In 2024, global health organizations continue to monitor and prevent Ebola outbreaks.

  • Improved vaccine distribution and containment strategies.
  • Focus on rapid response to prevent future outbreaks.
